It gets so cold in the winter, we have pack ice on the ocean!

It sure is cold here, but that doesn't take away from our winter traditions!

Enjoy a local favorite to warm up! Seal flipper pie has been a Newfoundland

favorite for decades. We also serve world famous poutine and keep the kettle

on so you can warm up after the day's adventures.

Explore the nooks and crannies and caves of the island!

Who doesn't love a great adventure? During the short summer months,

many locals take to the cliffs for some easy hiking. From various points

on the island, enjoy breathtaking views that are only reserved for North

Eastern Canada. Canada is renowned for the beuty of it's nature.

Savor the history of the island!

Newfoundland has a rich culture, so it isn't surprising that we have

our own unique phrases and distinct accents, reminiscent of the Irish.

Join the by's (guys) for a tour around our towns and museums. Learn about

our great love of the ocean and our fishing history. We were discovered in

1497 by John Cabot, who commented on how you could throw a net in the water

and immediately pull it up, brimming with fish.
